         ORDER EXTENSION OF MOVEMENT CONTROL
         CONDITIONAL (PKPB)

         As announced by the Honorable Prime
         Special Message from the Minister during his earlier, Order
         Movement Control Conditional or PKPB ruling
         until 12 May 2020, will be extended until June 9, 2020,
         ie for four (4) weeks.

         As stated by the Honorable Prime
         Minister, the implementation PKPB was running smoothly
         and regularly. Some sectors of the economy have also been opened.
         They were stranded at home or in places
         Elsewhere, including the students have come back to
         each house. It was found that the average trader, employers and


        people have been taking active efforts to comply with the Standard
        Operating Procedure (SOP).

        Therefore, the Ministry of Health welcomes the announcement of the extension
        Conditional Motion Control Order (PKPB) until June 9
        2020. It will continue to give space to the Government in
        COVID-19 combat the spread of this up to a higher level
        controlled, while balancing economic sectors opening
        carefully. The effectiveness of these measures will be assessed
        from time to time.

        MOH will intensify its efforts to reduce the positive case
        COVID-19 in Malaysia through active case detection (Active Case
        Detection) and focused approach (targeted approach), namely
        detection, screening and testing of target groups
        high risk. These actions can be carried out more
        through effective implementation Movement Control Order
        Conditional (PKPB) and the Movement Control Order
        Bolted (PKPD)

        OTHMAN ROAD MARKET UNDER CONTROL ORDER
        MOVEMENT tightened (PKPD)

        MOH took note of the announcement by the Minister of the Right
        (Cluster Security), in which the Jalan Othman, Petaling
        Jaya, Selangor were placed under the command of the Order
        Bolted Movement Control (PKPD). this command
        implemented following the detection of 27 positive cases COVID-19 in
        localities (including one (1) case outside the area).
        PKPD order involves three (3) zones and will be effective
        from 10 May 2020 until 23 May 2020.


        CENTERED APPROACH ACTIVITIES (TARGETED APPROACH)
        SHOWS POSITIVE RESULTS

        So far the implementation of activities focused approach (targeted
        approach) have a positive impact in detecting


       more cases COVID-19. As of May 10, 2020, MOH
       identified several target groups as follows:

         • Participants gathering in Seri Petaling and close contacts
          them;
         • Students and staff of the school and related tahfiz
          meeting with the assembly in Seri Petaling;
         • Malaysians returning from abroad and
          placed in quarantine centers;
         • Members of the MOH;
         • Population in localities PKPD;
         • Wholesale Market Kuala Lumpur (BKL) and markets
          BKL closely related;
         • Foreign workers at a construction site in the red zone; and
         • elderly care homes.

       People should not be concerned with pengingkatan new cases
       follow-up approach focused on an aggressive and proactive by
       Government because it shows the effectiveness of the activities
       This is carried out. The cases detected is absolutely isolated

       RELATED HEALTH ADVICE COVID-19

       MOH please cooperation of all levels and walks of life
       in the fight against the epidemic COVID-19. People need to take
       social responsibility (social responsibility) and have
       Self-control (self-regulation). Ministry of Health would like to advise people
       public to continue practicing the new norm in life:

       1) Adhere to the PKPB
       2) getting out of the house, except for matters that
         important
       3) Adopt a safe social (social distancing) at
         least 1 meter from other people
       4) Adopt an optimal level of cleanliness at all
         time, such as frequent hand washing using water
         and soap.
